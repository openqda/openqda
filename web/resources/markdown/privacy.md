## § 2 Collection of Personal Data When Using OpenQDA

**(1)** When using OpenQDA, we collect the personal data described below to enable the convenient use of the features, which
are technically necessary for us to offer you the functions of OpenQDA and to ensure stability and security (legal
basis: Art. 6 Paragraph 1 Sentence 1 lit. f GDPR):

- IP address
- Date and time of the request
- Time zone difference to Greenwich Mean Time (GMT)
- Content of the request (specific page)
- Access status/HTTP status code
- Amount of data transferred
- Website from which the request originates
- Browser
- Operating system and its interface
- Language and version of the browser software

**(2)** In addition to the aforementioned data, cookies are stored on your device when using OpenQDA. Cookies are small text
files that are stored in your device's memory and associated with the application you are using. Cookies allow certain
information to flow to the entity that sets the cookie (here: us). Cookies cannot execute programs or transmit viruses
to your device. They serve to make OpenQDA more user-friendly and effective overall.

- **a)** OpenQDA uses the following types of cookies, whose scope and functionality are explained below:
    - Transient cookies (see b)
    - Persistent cookies (see c)
- **b)** Transient cookies are automatically deleted when you close OpenQDA. These include session cookies in
  particular. They store a so-called session ID, which allows various requests to be assigned. This enables your device
  to be recognized when you use OpenQDA again. Session cookies are deleted when you log out or close the browser.
- **c)** Persistent cookies are automatically deleted after a specified duration, which may vary depending on the
  cookie. You can configure your operating system settings according to your preferences. Please note that you may not
  be able to use all functions of OpenQDA.

## § 3 Registration and Use of OpenQDA

**(1)** To grant you access to OpenQDA, you need to register with us by providing certain personal data (legal 
basis: Art. 6) Paragraph 1 Sentence 1 lit. b GDPR). The mandatory registration data includes:
- Name, which can be a pseudonym,
- Email address,
- Password

**(2)** We use the login credentials to authenticate you during login. The data you provide during registration will be
processed and used by us to (1) verify your authorization to use the platform; (2) enforce the terms of use of OpenQDA
and all associated rights and obligations; and (3) contact you to send you technical or legal notices, updates, security
alerts, or other messages (legal basis: Art. 6 Paragraph 1 Sentence 1 lit. f GDPR).  

**(3)** Additional user data that may be stored by you includes content that you voluntarily provide as part of your 
use of
OpenQDA (legal basis: Art. 6 Paragraph 1 Sentence 1 lit. a) GDPR). This may include:

- Voluntary personal information,
- Profile picture,
- Personal settings and configurations,
- Affiliation with created workgroups,
- Content created within OpenQDA (e.g., notes, codes, memos, documents),
- Interaction data with other users (e.g., comments, messages),
- Uploaded files and documents.

## § 4 Disclosure of Data

**(1)** Your personal data will not be disclosed to third parties for purposes other than those listed in the following
paragraph.  

**(2)** We only disclose your personal data to third parties if:

- **a)** You have given your explicit consent (Art. 6 Paragraph 1 Sentence 1 lit. a GDPR),
- **b)** The disclosure is necessary for the establishment, exercise, or defense of legal claims and there is no reason
  to assume that you have an overriding legitimate interest in the non-disclosure of your data (Art. 6 Paragraph 1
  Sentence 1 lit. f GDPR),
- **c)** There is a legal obligation for disclosure pursuant to Art. 6 Paragraph 1 Sentence 1 lit. c GDPR, and
- **d)** It is legally permissible and necessary for the processing of contractual relationships with you (Art. 6
  Paragraph 1 Sentence 1 lit. b GDPR).

## § 6 Integration of Vimeo Videos

**(1)** The teaching materials provided in OpenQDA may include Vimeo videos for illustrating teaching content, which are
stored on [http://www.vimeo.com](http://www.vimeo.com) and can be played directly from OpenQDA (legal basis: Art. 6
Paragraph 1 Sentence 1 lit. f GDPR). According to Vimeo, cookies are used to collect video statistics, improve
user-friendliness, and prevent abusive practices. For more information on the purpose and scope of data collection and
processing by Vimeo, please refer to Vimeo's privacy policy. There, you will also find further information about your
rights and settings to protect your privacy: [https://vimeo.com/privacy](https://vimeo.com/privacy). Vimeo also
processes your personal data in the USA and has subjected itself to the EU-US Privacy
Shield, [https://www.privacyshield.gov/Eu-US-Framework](https://www.privacyshield.gov/Eu-US-Framework).  

**(2)** The videos embedded by us, hosted on vimeo.com, are all provided with a "dnt" (do not track) attribute to minimize
the transmission of personal data to Vimeo.

## § 8 Newsletter

**(1)** With your consent, you can subscribe to our newsletter, through which we provide you with current updates about
OpenQDA.  

**(2)** To register for our newsletter, we use the so-called double opt-in procedure. This means that after your
registration, we send you an email to the specified email address in which we ask you to confirm that you wish to
receive the newsletter. If you do not confirm your registration within 24 hours, your information will be locked and
automatically deleted after one month. Additionally, we store your IP addresses and the times of registration and
confirmation. The purpose of the procedure is to prove your registration and, if necessary, to clarify any possible
misuse of your personal data.  

**(3)** The only mandatory information for sending the newsletter is your email address. Providing additional, separately
marked data is voluntary and is used to address you personally. After your confirmation, we store your email address for
the purpose of sending the newsletter (legal basis: Art. 6 Paragraph 1 Sentence 1 lit. a GDPR).  

**(4)** You can revoke your consent to receive the newsletter at any time and unsubscribe from the newsletter. You can
declare the revocation by clicking on the link provided in every newsletter email or by
emailing [openqda@uni-bremen.de](mailto:openqda@uni-bremen.de).

## § 9 Calendar

The content of the calendar offered in OpenQDA can be accessed and downloaded as an .ics file to import calendar entries
into your calendar, send entries to other users, or publish and share entries (legal basis: Art. 6 Paragraph 1 Sentence
1 lit. a GDPR).

## § 10 SSL Encryption

The connection to OpenQDA is secured with SSL encryption. When SSL encryption is activated, it is almost impossible for
third parties to read the data you transmit to us.

## § 11 Your Rights

**(1)** You have the following rights regarding your personal data:

- Right to access (Art. 15 GDPR),
- Right to rectification (Art. 16 GDPR) or erasure (Art. 17 GDPR),
- Right to restriction of processing (Art. 18 GDPR),
- Right to data portability (Art. 20 GDPR).

**(2)** You also have the right to lodge a complaint with a data protection supervisory authority regarding the processing
of your personal data by us (Art. 77 GDPR).

## § 12 Objection or Withdrawal of Consent to Data Processing

**(1)** If you have given your consent to the processing of your data, you can withdraw it at any time (Art. 7 Paragraph 3
GDPR). Such a withdrawal affects the admissibility of the processing of your personal data after you have expressed it
to us.

**(2)** If we base the processing of your personal data on a balancing of interests (Art. 6 Paragraph 1 Sentence 1 lit. f
GDPR), you can object to the processing of your personal data (Art. 21 GDPR), provided there are reasons arising from
your particular situation or the objection is directed against direct marketing. In the latter case, you have a general
right to object, which will be implemented by us without specifying a particular situation.  

**(3)** If you wish to exercise your right of withdrawal or objection, an email
to [openqda@uni-bremen.de](mailto:openqda@uni-bremen.de) is sufficient.

## § 13 Changes to This Privacy Policy

Due to the further development of OpenQDA or changes in legal or regulatory requirements, it may become necessary to
amend this privacy policy. The current privacy policy is always available within OpenQDA.
